What this experiment was

A smoke test. The whole point: confirm the pipeline closes end-to-end — VAE compresses 9-channel rasters → Sketcher (DiT) paints denoised codes in latent space → Inker (autoregressive transformer) reads the painted blueprint via cross-attention and writes vector tokens → eval harness measures everything — without anything blowing up. We are not testing whether the generated cities look good yet; that's Experiments 1-4.

Training loss curves

All three stages trained without divergence. Loss curves are smooth, no NaNs.

VAE — 50 epochs reconstruction (BCE on binary channels + MSE on density + KL regulariser)

Stage A — DiT diffusion (1 epoch in latent space, EDM noise)

Stage B — Inker (1 epoch teacher-forcing, cross-attn to ground-truth raster)

What the synthetic training tiles look like

5,000 synthetic tiles fed the smoke test. Each tile is 2 km × 2 km (512×512 pixels at 4 m/px), encoded as a 9-channel raster. Two density modes: sparse (a few buildings + 2-3 short roads) and dense (8×8 grid roads + diagonal cross-roads + ~64 buildings + landuse polygons + POIs). Each panel below is one channel of the raster; black = mask off, white = mask on; channel 5 (building density, viridis) is continuous.
